§ 01

What I do

EdTech is uniquely hard for AI. A demo can wow an investor in twelve seconds and still fail a sixth-grader on Tuesday morning. The market is flooded with AI tutors that hallucinate, AI graders teachers don't trust, and AI content nobody finishes.

I help founders cut through that noise. I work alongside your team to identify AI use cases that learners and educators actually adopt, validate them before you over-build, and scale the ones that earn their place in the workflow.

The work concentrates on three things:

  1. Identify defensible AI use cases tied to learning outcomes, teacher time saved, and unit economics—not engagement vanity metrics.
  2. Validate with real classrooms, learners, or L&D buyers before pouring engineering into the wrong feature.
  3. Build evaluation frameworks that include pedagogical efficacy, hallucination tolerance, age-appropriate safety, and cost-per-learner.
An early-stage K–12 platform wanted an "AI tutor" to differentiate. We instead found teachers were burning four-plus hours weekly generating differentiated practice. We shipped a narrow teacher copilot, validated it across eight schools, and within 90 days teacher weekly-active doubled and three districts expanded mid-contract.

§ 03

When to call

EdTech AI is moving faster than school procurement cycles. Speed without clarity creates expensive product debt. Reach out when momentum feels high but outcomes feel uncertain.

  • You're juggling multiple AI ideas—tutor, content gen, grading, analytics—without a prioritization framework.
  • The MVP works in a demo, but teachers don't reach for it on a Tuesday morning.
  • LLM costs are climbing with usage but not with revenue, and unit economics are getting fuzzy.
  • Product, engineering, and GTM disagree on what "AI success" means—engagement? grades? retention?
  • Your differentiation is "we use AI"—and so is every pitch deck in your category.
  • FERPA, COPPA, district procurement, or academic-integrity concerns are slowing deals you thought were closed.
§ 04

How I work

Hypothesis-driven. Evidence-based. Built around what actually changes behavior in classrooms and learning workflows—not what plays well on stage.

  1. Diagnostic. Audit current use cases, data readiness (including student-data privacy), educator and learner workflows, and unit economics.
  2. Prioritization. Identify one or two high-leverage AI bets tied to measurable outcomes—learning gains, teacher time saved, completion, or expansion revenue.
  3. MVP design. Define narrow scope, success metrics, an evaluation framework that includes pedagogical efficacy, and a rollout plan that respects educator trust.
  4. Scale. Implement feedback loops, cost controls, observability, safety review, and roadmap sequencing as you move from pilot to production.

I work alongside your team. I do not replace it. The goal is to leave you with durable systems and sharper conviction—not dependency.
