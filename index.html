§ 01

What I do

I help EdTech founders move from "we should use AI" to measurable learning and business outcomes. Most teams either overbuild too early or ship AI demos that never translate into classroom adoption or revenue.

I focus on identifying the right use cases, validating product-market fit with real learners and educators, and building scalable AI systems that hold up in production.

I design AI product systems that do three things:

  1. Identify defensible AI use cases tied to learning outcomes, teacher time saved, and unit economics.
  2. Validate demand and usage signals with real learners and educators before over-investing in engineering.
  3. Scale MVPs into production-grade systems with the right data, feedback loops, governance, and student-data privacy posture.

I treat use case discovery, PMF validation, and technical scaling as one connected system. When aligned, your AI becomes a durable advantage instead of a feature experiment.

An early-stage K-12 platform wanted to "add AI" for differentiation. Instead of building a generic tutor, we identified that teachers were spending 4+ hours per week generating differentiated practice. We prototyped a focused teacher copilot, validated usage with 8 pilot schools, and shipped a narrow MVP. Within 90 days, teacher weekly active doubled and 3 districts expanded mid-contract.

§ 03

When to call

EdTech AI is moving faster than school procurement cycles. Speed without clarity creates waste. Reach out if momentum feels high but outcomes feel uncertain.

Common signals:

  • You're experimenting with multiple AI ideas (tutor, content gen, grading, analytics) but lack prioritization discipline.
  • Your MVP works technically but teachers and students don't engage deeply.
  • Model costs are rising without proportional revenue impact.
  • Product, engineering, and GTM are misaligned on what "AI success" means.
  • You're unsure whether your differentiation is defensible or replicable.
  • FERPA, COPPA, district procurement, or academic integrity concerns are slowing deals.
§ 04

How I work

My work is hypothesis-driven, evidence-based, and execution-focused. I combine AI capability understanding with rigorous product discipline.

Typical engagement structure:

  1. Diagnostic. Audit use cases, data readiness, educator and learner workflows, student-data privacy posture, and economics.
  2. Prioritization. Identify 1-2 high-leverage AI opportunities tied to measurable outcomes: learning gains, teacher time saved, completion, or retention.
  3. MVP design. Define narrow scope, success metrics, evaluation framework (including pedagogical efficacy), and rollout plan.
  4. Scale. Implement feedback loops, cost controls, observability, and roadmap sequencing as you move from pilot to production.

I work alongside your team. I do not replace it. The goal is to leave you with durable systems, not dependency.
